## Part 1 — Scriptural Grounding for the Waqf Itself

### 1.1 The Khaybar Waqf of ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb (raḍiyallāhu ʿanhu)

The seminal hadith for the institution of waqf is found in **Sahih al-Bukhari
Book 50, Number 895** (also Book 51, Number 33):

> "Umar bin Khattab got some land in Khaibar and he went to the Prophet to
> consult him about it saying, 'O Allah's Apostle, I got some land in Khaibar
> better than which I have never had, what do you suggest that I do with it?'
> The Prophet said, 'If you like you can give the land as endowment and give
> its fruits in charity.' So Umar gave it in charity as an endowment on the
> condition that **it would not be sold nor given to anybody as a present
> and not to be inherited**, but its yield would be given in charity to the
> poor people, to the kith and kin, for freeing slaves, for Allah's Cause,
> to the travelers and guests; and that **there would be no harm if the
> guardian of the endowment ate from it according to his need with good
> intention, and fed others without storing it for the future**."

This single hadith establishes the four operative principles the design doc
relies on:

| Principle | Doc reference | Hadith basis |
|---|---|---|
| Inalienability of the corpus | §6.1a (Holdings: "inalienable by design") | "would not be sold nor given to anybody as a present and not to be inherited" |
| Dedication of usufruct to a defined beneficiary class | §3 (Stakeholder map: "Beneficiaries — Muslim families in the GTA"), §6.1c (Charitable entity) | "yield would be given in charity to the poor people, to the kith and kin, for freeing slaves, for Allah's Cause, to the travelers and guests" |
| Legitimacy of an appointed trustee (mutawalli) | §4.1 (Mutawalli / Board of Trustees) | "the guardian of the endowment" |
| Permissibility of trustee compensation in moderation | §4.1 (open question on trustee compensation) | "no harm if the guardian of the endowment ate from it according to his need with good intention" |

### 1.2 Sadaqah Jāriyah — Why Waqf Outlasts the Founder

**Sahih Muslim Book 13, Number 4005:**

> "When a man dies, his acts come to an end, but three: recurring charity
> (ṣadaqah jāriyah), or knowledge (by which people) benefit, or a pious son
> who prays for him."

A waqf is the institutional form of "recurring charity": the principal asset
is preserved while its usufruct continuously provides ongoing charitable
benefit long after the founder's passing. This grounds the design doc's claim
in §6.5 that "the legal entities are vehicles; the Waqf is the covenant"
that outlasts founders, governance changes, and corporate forms.

### 1.3 Other Prophetic Endorsements of Endowed Assets

- **Abu Talha's Garden (Bairuha):** When the Quranic verse urging believers
  to spend from what they love was revealed, Abu Talha endowed his most
  prized date-palm garden, Bairuha, for Allah's sake. The Prophet ﷺ praised
  it as "profitable property" and instructed him to distribute the usufruct
  among his relatives (Sahih al-Bukhari Book 38 No. 511; Book 51 No. 30).
- **Saʿd bin ʿUbāda's Garden (Al-Makhraf):** Saʿd dedicated his garden as an
  ongoing endowment on behalf of his deceased mother, with the Prophet's
  approval (Sahih al-Bukhari Book 51 Nos. 19 & 24).
- **An unnamed companion's garden:** A man asked if charity would benefit
  his deceased mother and dedicated his garden in her name (Sahih
  al-Bukhari Book 51 No. 31).

These corroborate the Khaybar precedent: dedicating land for ongoing
usufruct, even on behalf of others, is a well-established prophetic practice.

## Part 2 — Trustee (Mutawalli) Conduct, Removal, Shūrā, and Accountability

**Maps to:** §4 (Roles & Decision Rights), §5 (Shūrā Process), §10.1 (open
question on trustee removal).

### 2.1 The Weight of Amānah (Trust)

**Three foundational verses:**

- **Al-Anfāl 8:27** — "O you who have believed, do not betray Allāh and the
  Messenger or betray your trusts while you know [the consequence]."
- **An-Nisāʾ 4:58** — "Indeed, Allāh commands you to render trusts to whom
  they are due and when you judge between people to judge with justice."
- **Al-Aḥzāb 33:72** — "Indeed, We offered the Trust to the heavens and the
  earth and the mountains, and they declined to bear it and feared it; but
  man [undertook to] bear it. Indeed, he was unjust and ignorant."

**Khiyānah (betrayal of trust) as a sign of hypocrisy:** The Prophet ﷺ taught
that among the signs of a hypocrite are: "Whenever he speaks, he tells a
lie. Whenever he promises, he always breaks it. **If you trust him, he proves
to be dishonest.**" (Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim, on the signs of the
munāfiq.)

### 2.2 Qualifications of a Trustee — Strength + Trustworthiness

**Al-Qaṣaṣ 28:26** — "Indeed, the best one you can hire is the strong (qawi)
and the trustworthy (amīn)."

**Sahih al-Bukhari** — When a man asked the Prophet ﷺ when the Hour would
occur, he replied: "When honesty is lost, then wait for the Hour." When asked
how, he answered: "**When the power or authority comes in the hands of unfit
persons, then wait for the Hour.**"

These two together set the bar for §4.1: the mutawalli must be *both*
competent (qawi — capable of discharging the role) and trustworthy (amīn —
of confirmed integrity). The Scholar Council attestation procedure proposed
in §4.1 is an attempt to operationalize the second prong.

### 2.3 Removal for Cause

The corpus does **not** contain a step-by-step legal procedure for trustee
removal (witness counts, hearing format, etc.). What it does establish:

- **Adherence to law as a condition of leadership:** Sahih Muslim notes the
  "justification for hating the amirs for violating the laws of the
  Sharīʿah."
- **Prophetic precedent for removal after public complaint and investigation:**
  ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb dismissed Saʿd from his leadership position in Kufa,
  dispatching investigators to ask the community about his conduct before
  finalizing his decision.
- **Evidentiary requirement:** "If people were to be given what they claim
  (without proving their claim) the life and property of the nation would be
  lost" — accusations require evidence or oath (Sahih al-Bukhari).

> **Notebook flag (verify externally):** *"Later Islamic jurisprudence
> (fiqh) explicitly codifies the judge's right to remove a mutawalli for
> manifest betrayal, gross negligence, or public sin based on these very
> principles."* The corpus does not contain this codification — it is an
> aside from the model that must be confirmed against fiqh works.

**What this means for §4.1's "Removal" subsection:**

- ✓ Grounds for removal (manifest betrayal, gross negligence, public sin)
  are scripturally and prophetically supported in principle.
- ✓ A formal investigation step before removal is supported by ʿUmar's
  precedent.
- ✓ An evidentiary standard (witnesses or oath) is required.
- ✗ The exact procedural form (quorum for removal vote, scholar attestation
  threshold, notice period) is **not** established in this corpus and must
  come from fiqh consultation.

### 2.4 Shūrā as Binding Practice

**Ash-Shūrā 42:38** — "And those who have responded to their Lord and
established prayer and **whose affair is [determined by] consultation among
themselves**, and from what We have provided them, they spend."

**Prophetic practice:** During the slander of ʿĀʾisha (raḍiyallāhu ʿanhā),
when revelation was delayed, the Prophet ﷺ "sent for ʿAlī and Usāma to
consult them." He actively gathered counsel before deciding even when
revelation was an option.

> **Notebook flag (verify externally):** *"Surah 3:159 ('and consult them
> in the matter'), while an explicit command to the Prophet to consult his
> companions, is not contained in the provided sources."* The corpus is
> Saheeh International and apparently does not surface this verse on the
> query — verify the wording in another translation.

**What this grounds:** Section 5's claim that shūrā binds the mutawalli on
material decisions is supported. The cadence (§5.2) and quorum (§5.3) are
operational specifics not addressed by the corpus and must be set by user
decision + scholar review.

### 2.5 Accountability — The Shepherd Hadith

**Sahih al-Bukhari and Sahih Muslim** — the well-known hadith of the
shepherd:

> "**All of you are guardians (kullukum rāʿin) and responsible for your
> charges.** The Ruler is a guardian and responsible for his subjects; and
> a man is a guardian of his family... and a servant is a guardian of the
> property of his master and is responsible for his charge."

And the stark warning:

> "**Allāh does not entrust to His bondsman the responsibility of managing
> the affairs of his subjects and he dies as a dishonest (ruler) but
> Paradise is forbidden by Allāh for such a (ruler).**"

These verses ground the §4.8 decision matrix's emphasis on accountable, named
authority for every decision class. Every trustee, every director, every
operational lead is a rāʿin answerable to Allāh ﷻ for the charges they hold.

## Part 3 — Ijārah, Riba, Gharar, Salam, and Documentation

**Maps to:** §6.4 (CSRA), §7.3 (debt and financing), §7.5 (riba and gharar
guardrails), §10.1 (open questions on lease ijārah-compliance, salam for CSRA).

### 3.1 Ethical Leasing and Fair Dealing

**Withholding wages:**

> "Allāh says, 'I will be an opponent to three types of people on the Day
> of Resurrection,'" one of which is "**one who employs a laborer and takes
> full work from him but does not pay him for his labour.**" (Sahih
> al-Bukhari Book 36 No. 470; Book 34 No. 430)

> **Notebook flag (verify externally):** *"The specific hadith 'Give the
> worker his wage before his sweat dries' is from outside the provided
> sources (Sunan Ibn Mājah)."* — the principle is in the corpus; the famous
> wording is not.

**Quranic standards on weights, measures, and fair dealing:**

- **Al-Muṭaffifīn 83:1–3** — "Woe to those who give less [than due]..."
- **Al-Anʿām 6:152** — "Give full measure and weight in justice."
- **Al-Isrāʾ 17:35** — "And give full measure when you measure, and weigh
  with an even balance. That is the best way and best in result."

**What this grounds:** The §6.1b lease (Holdings → Operating) must be at
fair market rent and clearly documented. Underpayment for the Waqf's benefit
of the Operating entity, or overpayment to drain the Operating entity, both
violate this principle.

### 3.2 Hīlah (Legal Stratagems) — The Most Cutting Warning

The Qur'an condemns the Sabbath-breakers who used a legal workaround
(setting nets on Friday, collecting on Sunday) to violate the spirit of the
Sabbath:

- **Al-Aʿrāf 7:163–166** — "Be apes, despised."
- **Al-Baqarah 2:65** — confirms the same punishment.

The Prophet ﷺ explicitly warned against legal workarounds:

> "**May Allāh curse the Jews, for Allāh made the fat (of animals) illegal
> for them, yet they melted the fat and sold it and ate its price.**"
> (Sahih al-Bukhari Book 34 No. 438; Sahih Muslim Book 10 No. 3840)

**What this grounds — and warns:** The tri-entity stack must not become
a hīlah for evading the Waqf restrictions. If Operating routinely runs at
"loss" so surplus never reaches Charitable; if rent is set to extract value
from Holdings; if "donations" are structured to return benefit to OGDEN —
each is a structural hīlah. **§4.8 (decision matrix) and §7.5 (riba/gharar
guardrails) must include explicit anti-hīlah review by the Scholar Council
on an annual basis.** This is a recommended addition to the design doc.

### 3.3 Riba

**Quranic prohibition (Al-Baqarah 2:275–279):**

> "Those who consume interest cannot stand [on the Day of Resurrection]
> except as one stands who is being beaten by Satan into insanity. That is
> because they say, 'Trade is [just] like interest.' But Allāh has permitted
> trade and has forbidden interest..."

The verses warn believers to "give up what remains [due to you] of interest"
or else "be informed of a war [against you] from Allāh and His Messenger."

**Categories of riba (riba al-faḍl):**

> "**The bartering of gold for silver is Riba except if it is from hand to
> hand and equal in amount, and wheat grain for wheat grain is usury except
> if it is from hand to hand and equal in amount, and dates for dates is
> usury...**" (Sahih al-Bukhari Book 34 No. 344; Sahih Muslim Book 10 No.
> 3853)

**Severity — extends to all participants:**

> The Prophet ﷺ "**cursed the accepter of interest and its payer, and one
> who records it, and the two witnesses, and he said: They are all equal.**"
> (Sahih Muslim Book 10 No. 3881)

**What this grounds:** §7.3's "default posture: avoid debt" is non-negotiable.
The cursing of the recorder and witnesses extends accountability to the
Operating directors and even to Charitable directors who knowingly facilitate
a riba transaction. Any financing instrument used must be attested by the
Scholar Council before execution (§7.3 already states this; this evidence
strengthens the requirement).

### 3.4 Gharar (Excessive Uncertainty)

> "**He who buys foodgrain should not sell it until he has taken possession
> of it.**" (Sahih Muslim Book 10 No. 3640)

> The Prophet ﷺ "**forbade the sale of fruits until their good condition
> becomes clear, and (the danger) of blight is no more.**" (Sahih Muslim
> Book 10 No. 3667; Sahih al-Bukhari Book 34 No. 399)

> Forbade the sale of *Habal al-Habala* — unborn animal offspring still in
> the womb (Sahih al-Bukhari Book 34 No. 353).

**Implication for CSRA — direct hit:** A naïve advance-purchase of "whatever
the farm produces this season" is structurally gharar-laden. **A CSRA
contribution that resembles this must be restructured.** §6.4 of the design
doc already identifies CSRA as needing scholar review; this corpus confirms
the concern is non-trivial.

### 3.5 Salam (Forward Sale) — The Permitted Exception

> When the Prophet ﷺ came to Madinah and farmers were taking advance
> payments for fruits to be delivered later, he permitted it but bound it
> by strict conditions: "**Whoever pays in advance the price of a thing to
> be delivered later should pay it for a specified measure at specified
> weight for a specified period.**" (Sahih al-Bukhari Book 35 Nos. 443 &
> 445)

**What this grounds — concrete CSRA design constraint:** For a CSRA advance
purchase to be valid as salam, the contract must specify:

1. The exact **measure or weight** of the produce delivered (e.g., "20 kg of
   tomatoes, 10 kg of squash" — not "a share of the harvest").
2. The exact **delivery date** (e.g., "first week of August" — not "when the
   harvest is ready").
3. The **price paid in full at the time of contract** (this is the defining
   feature of salam).

**Recommendation for the design doc §6.4:** Add a sentence stating that
"CSRA advance-purchase contributions must be structured as salam with
specified weight, measure, and delivery date per the conditions of Sahih
al-Bukhari Book 35 Nos. 443 & 445. Generic 'share of the harvest' models
are not permissible without further scholar review."

### 3.6 Documentation and Witnessing

**Al-Baqarah 2:282** (the longest verse in the Qur'an):

> "O you who have believed, when you contract a debt for a specified term,
> write it down. And let a scribe write [it] between you in justice... And
> bring to witness two witnesses from among your men..."

**Sahih al-Bukhari Book 48 No. 837:** "**Produce your two witnesses or else
the defendant is to take an oath.**"

**What this grounds:** §5.4 ("Documentation") is scripturally mandated, not
optional. Annual financial transparency reports, documented shūrā decisions,
and witnessed contracts (lease, grants, CSRA salam contracts) are all
required by 2:282.

## Part 4 — What This Corpus Cannot Resolve

The following open questions from §10.1 of the design doc are **not**
substantively answered by Qur'an + Sahihayn alone and require either a
specialized fiqh corpus or live scholar consultation:

| Question | Why this corpus is insufficient |
|---|---|
| Madhhab posture on the operative definition of Waqf | Madhhab differences are codified in fiqh works (e.g., al-Mughnī, al-Bidāyat al-Mujtahid), not in Qur'an + Sahihayn alone |
| Cash-waqf (waqf al-nuqūd) permissibility and conditions | Contemporary issue; resolved through later fiqh and contemporary fatwā councils (e.g., AAOIFI, Majmaʿ al-Fiqh al-Islāmī) |
| Specific ijārah requirements between related entities | Requires contemporary Islamic finance standards (AAOIFI Sharīʿah Standards) |
| Murābaḥah / mushārakah / diminishing mushārakah specifics | Same — contemporary Islamic finance scholarship |
| Trustee removal procedural form (quorum, notice, etc.) | Requires fiqh codification beyond the principles in the corpus |
| Whether founding members can be both wāqif-adjacent and beneficiaries | Madhhab-specific (some madhāhib permit waqf ʿalā al-nafs, others restrict it) |

**Recommended next step:** Use this grounding document as the *Islamic
foundation* portion of the brief presented to a fiqh-competent scholar.
The scholar's role is then to (a) confirm the corpus-grounded findings
above, and (b) extend them to the contemporary issues this corpus cannot
resolve.
