# MTC Founding-Member Covenant — Review

> **What this is:** The commitment instrument that a founding household
> signs during the pre-land window of MTC. It precedes — and will be
> superseded or absorbed by — the formal deed of waqf and the Charitable
> entity's bylaws once those are drafted by counsel and attested by the
> Scholar Council.

> **What this is not:** A legal contract. Not bylaws. Not a residency
> agreement. Not a sale of equity, units, or shares — those do not exist
> and never will, per the locked governance design at
> [stages/design-mtc-waqf-governance-review.md](design-mtc-waqf-governance-review.md).
> Not a fundraising solicitation; the figures inside any specific
> founding-member's commitment are documented per-household, never
> published.

## 1. Purpose & Scope

### What this instrument does

This covenant is the document a founding household signs to be **seated
as a founding member of the Moontrance Collective** during the pre-land
window — the period running from now until the deed of waqf is executed
at land acquisition (per
[stages/design-mtc-waqf-governance-review.md §9.1–9.2](design-mtc-waqf-governance-review.md)).

It captures three things in writing:

1. The household's commitment to MTC's Waqf-framed structure and the
   character standards that flow from it.
2. The form, but not the figures, of any capital contribution the
   household offers.
3. The mutually agreed rights the household receives in return —
   explicitly excluding equity, residency, or any tradeable interest.

### What this instrument is not

- **Not a legal contract.** Counsel will draft binding instruments
  later; this covenant binds in conscience and in shūrā record, and
  serves as the working document until those legal instruments exist.
- **Not bylaws.** It does not govern the entities; the deed of waqf
  and entity-level bylaws will do that.
- **Not a residency agreement.** Residency is a separate post-land
  application (per design doc §3, founding-member-vs-resident decision
  resolved 2026-05-02).
- **Not a fundraising solicitation.** Specific dollar figures, schedules,
  or terms are documented per-household in a private commitment letter
  attached as Schedule A; this covenant text is the standing public
  template.

### Audience

- **Founding households** — the signers.
- **OGDEN** as wāqif and initial trustee — the counter-signer.
- **The Scholar Council** — for attestation of the covenant template
  before any founding-member is enrolled.
- **Future legal counsel** — as the working brief from which the
  formal post-land instruments are drafted.

## 2. Foundational Frame

### Why a covenant before there is a Waqf

A waqf requires three things: a *corpus* (the asset endowed), a *wāqif*
(the founder), and a *purpose* (the beneficiary class and intended
benefit). MTC has the wāqif (OGDEN, per design doc §8.1) and the purpose
(Muslim families in the GTA / Halton region encountering the Collective
and Community), but the corpus does not yet exist — the land has not
been acquired.

In that pre-corpus window, the institution exists only as a covenant
between the wāqif and the people who have committed to it. The Khaybar
hadith (Sahih al-Bukhari Book 50 No. 895, quoted in design doc §2)
establishes that ʿUmar consulted the Prophet ﷺ *before* the endowment
was formalized — the consultation and the intention preceded the
instrument. This covenant is the analogous instrument: the founding
household and the wāqif consult, agree, and document, before the deed
of waqf can be executed.

### The Jamāʿah anchor

The Jamāʿah pillar of the Collective
([src/data/module-overviews/collective-overview.js](../src/data/module-overviews/collective-overview.js))
names governance-by-consultation and shared accountability as
non-optional conditions. A founding member is the human correlate of
this principle: a person who is willing to be in shūrā with others,
under the same standards of qawī + amīn that the design doc §4.1
applies to trustees, before there is land or formal authority on which
to exercise either.

### Sadaqah jāriyah and the founding household

Sahih Muslim Book 13 No. 4005 (also in design doc §2) names recurring
charity as one of the three things whose reward continues after a
person's death. A founding member's contribution — whether time,
expertise, capital, or all three — is offered with the explicit
intention that it be sadaqah jāriyah: that it persist beyond their own
participation, beyond their own life, beyond the founding generation.
This intention shapes everything below. **[scholar review]**

## 3. Definition of a Founding Member

### Who qualifies

A **founding member** is a Muslim household that has:

a. Made a documented covenant commitment (this instrument, signed and
   witnessed per §8);
b. Been seated by OGDEN as the initial wāqif, after Scholar Council
   attestation of the household's qawī + amīn standing;
c. Done both of the above **before the seating of the third independent
   (non-OGDEN) trustee** of the mutawalli, which is the structural close
   of the founding window.

After the third independent trustee is seated, no new founding members
may be enrolled. New committed households at that point are
**residency applicants**, **Charitable donors**, **qard ḥasan lenders**,
or any combination — all valid statuses, none of them "founding."

### When enrollment opens

Per [website/mtc/journey/index.html:247](../website/mtc/journey/index.html),
founding-member enrollment opens when at least one primary funding
channel reaches M2 — a signed agreement or awarded grant, not a stated
intent. This rule is preserved here: the covenant cannot be signed
until the M2 gate is met. **[open question]:** confirm that this gate
applies to founding-member enrollment generally, not only to the first
founding member.

### Soft cap on count

**[open question]:** Recommended default is a soft cap set in shūrā at
the time of enrollment opening — a number small enough that
"founding member" carries weight, large enough that the founding
generation can absorb the work of the pre-land window. Suggested
starting figure for shūrā discussion: **seven to twelve households.**
The cap is revisable in shūrā but not unbounded.

### What founding-member status is not

- It is **not residency.** A founding member who later wishes to live
  on the land applies through the residency gate like anyone else
  (design doc §3, resolved 2026-05-02).
- It is **not equity.** It conveys no ownership of any entity (design
  doc §6.4 + §7.3 — equity is structurally prohibited).
- It is **not a sale, advance-purchase, or pre-sale of any kind.**
  Islam does not permit the sale of what one does not yet possess
  (*bayʿ mā laysa ʿindak*); the pre-land MTC has no productive corpus
  capable of delivery, and so no advance-purchase contract may be
  offered against it. Capital flows are restricted to the forms in §6.
- It is **not tradeable, transferable, or inheritable.** The
  designation attaches to the household that signed it. If the
  signatories pass away, the designation is honoured posthumously
  but does not pass to heirs as a controlling interest. Heirs may of
  course apply to MTC in their own right.

## 4. What the Founding Member Commits To

### 4.1 Character standard — qawī + amīn

The founding member affirms that they will conduct themselves to the
standard articulated in **Al-Qaṣaṣ 28:26** — *qawī* (capable of
discharging the role they have accepted) and *amīn* (of confirmed
integrity). This is the same standard the governance design doc §4.1
applies to trustees, and it is applied here because founding members
are eligible for mutawalli candidacy and because their conduct in the
pre-land window models the conduct expected of all future stewards.

The Prophetic warning that *"when honesty is lost, then wait for the
Hour... when the power or authority comes in the hands of unfit
persons, then wait for the Hour"* (Sahih al-Bukhari, design doc §4.1)
is the converse case the founding member commits to avoid embodying.

### 4.2 Participation in shūrā

The founding member commits to **active, documented participation in
the pre-land mutawalli shūrā** convened by OGDEN. Per design doc §5.2,
the cadence is quarterly plus called sessions. **[open question]:**
the floor — recommended at majority attendance per rolling four-quarter
window — is documented in Schedule A so it can be evaluated for the
removal-for-cause process in §7.

### 4.3 Capital contribution (form, not figure)

The founding member commits to one or more of the capital-contribution
forms enumerated in §6. The specific figure, schedule, and contribution
type are documented per-household in the private commitment letter
(Schedule A), not in this covenant text.

### 4.4 Time and expertise commitment

In addition to shūrā attendance, the founding member commits to
contributing time or expertise during the pre-land window in a domain
relevant to the founding work — e.g., land scouting, legal liaison,
masjid-network outreach, scholar engagement, financial planning,
construction, agriculture, education, hospitality, technology. The
specific commitment is named in Schedule A.

### 4.5 Confidentiality during pre-acquisition

**[open question]:** During the pre-land window, sensitive negotiations
will arise (parcel candidates, lease terms, scholar engagement). The
founding member commits to keeping such matters confidential to the
shūrā until OGDEN authorizes disclosure. **Recommended scope:** a
narrow clause covering pre-acquisition negotiations only, expiring
on land closing or three years from signing, whichever is earlier.

### 4.6 Conduct standards

The founding member affirms that they will not:

- Hold themselves out publicly as an owner, equity-holder, member of a
  for-profit, or beneficiary of an investment in MTC. Such framing is
  prohibited (design doc §6.4 / §7.3).
- Solicit funds for MTC outside the channels and forms agreed with the
  mutawalli — to protect against unauthorized fundraising and the
  riba/gharar/hīlah failure modes the governance design is built to
  prevent (design doc §7.5).
- Attempt to engineer benefit to themselves or to related parties from
  inter-entity transactions, hiring decisions, lease terms, or
  beneficiary-program designations. Conflicts of interest are disclosed
  to the mutawalli and recused. **[scholar review]**

## 5. What the Founding Member Receives

The founding member receives the following — and only the following —
in exchange for their commitment.

### 5.1 Permanent founding-member designation

Recognition by name (or by household, where preferred) in the records
of MTC and in the deed of waqf when it is executed. The designation
is permanent in the sense that it cannot be revoked except by the
removal process in §7 or by the founding member's voluntary
withdrawal — but it is **not** a controlling interest, and it
carries no claim against the corpus.

### 5.2 Shūrā voice during the pre-land and early-residency windows

The founding member sits in the pre-land mutawalli shūrā, contributes
to material decisions on a one-household-one-voice basis, and has
their position recorded in writing per §8.

After the deed of waqf is executed and the mutawalli is formally
seated, the founding-member designate (per design doc §4.1) doubles
as a provisional resident voice until the resident community is
seated, at which point the founding-member shūrā role transitions to
that of an advisory body convened by the mutawalli at least annually.
**[scholar review]** — the post-residency advisory transition.

### 5.3 Priority consideration for residency

Once land is acquired and the residency application process opens, a
founding member who applies for residency receives **priority
consideration**, not a guarantee. Residency is gated by the residency
covenant's own qualifications (which are out of scope here). A
founding member who is not a resident retains all other founding-member
rights regardless.

### 5.4 Eligibility for mutawalli candidacy

The founding member is eligible to be nominated to a mutawalli seat
when one becomes vacant, subject to the qualifications and process
in design doc §4.1 (qawī + amīn, Scholar Council attestation,
staggered three-year terms with renewal limits). Eligibility is not a
guaranteed seat; the mutawalli composition rules govern actual
appointments.

### 5.5 Eligibility for Charitable entity board membership

The founding member is eligible for nomination to the Charitable
entity's board (when the entity is registered, per design doc §10.3),
on the same eligibility-not-guarantee basis as §5.4.

### 5.6 Tax receipts where applicable

To the extent the founding member's capital contribution flows through
the Charitable entity (per §6 below) and is structured as a qualifying
charitable gift, tax receipts will be issued under the Charitable
entity's registration. **[lawyer review]** — receipts cannot be issued
before the Charitable entity is registered; the timing of registration
relative to founding-member enrollment is itself open (design doc
§10.3).

### 5.7 What the founding member does NOT receive

- **No equity** in any entity, no shares, no units, no membership
  interest in a for-profit form.
- **No guaranteed residency** on the land.
- **No tradeable, transferable, or inheritable status.** Founding-member
  designation is personal to the household that signed.
- **No control** over the Waqf, the corpus, the entities, the
  beneficiary class, or the surplus split. Voice, yes; control, no.
- **No claim against the corpus.** If the founding member withdraws or
  is removed, no portion of any prior contribution becomes a debt the
  Waqf or any entity owes them — except qard ḥasan repayment per its
  own terms (§6.2 below).

## 6. Capital Contribution Framework

This section enumerates the **only permitted forms** by which a
founding member may direct capital toward MTC. Any other form requires
Scholar Council attestation before it may be entered into.
**[scholar review]**

### 6.1 No advance-purchase or pre-sale of any kind

The classical exception to *bayʿ mā laysa ʿindak* is **salam** —
advance-payment for goods of specified measure, weight, and delivery
date (Sahih al-Bukhari Book 35 Nos. 443 & 445). Salam is **not
available** to MTC during the pre-land window: there is no productive
corpus, no farm, no Operating entity standing capable of delivery, and
nothing therefore that satisfies the prophetic conditions. Even
post-acquisition, MTC will not offer salam-style advance-purchase as
a public capital channel — see governance doc §6.4.

A founding member's capital therefore enters MTC only via §6.2
(qard ḥasan), §6.3 (charitable donation), §6.4 (restricted donation
for capital improvements), or §6.5 (in-kind). No exceptions.
**[scholar review]**

### 6.2 Qard ḥasan (interest-free loan)

A loan to the Operating, Holdings, or Charitable entity (whichever is
appropriate to the use), repayable in full per a documented schedule,
with **no interest, no profit-share, no equity conversion, no preferred
return, no late fees structured as additional principal**.
**[scholar review]** — to confirm the structure is not a hīlah for
riba.

- **Repayment terms:** Documented in Schedule A.
- **Forgiveness:** A founding member may at any point convert
  outstanding qard ḥasan principal into a charitable donation (§6.3),
  with appropriate tax-receipt treatment if Charitable is the
  borrower; this conversion is voluntary and never assumed.

### 6.3 Charitable donation

An unrestricted gift to the Charitable entity (when registered),
qualifying for tax receipt where applicable. No expectation of return,
no restriction on use, no claim against the corpus.

### 6.4 Restricted charitable donation for capital improvements

A restricted gift to the Charitable entity, designated for a specific
capital improvement on the land (e.g., masjid construction, water
system, residency-housing structure). The Charitable entity receives
the gift, applies the restriction, and grants the funds to Holdings
for the named purpose. **[lawyer review]** — restricted-donation
routing under Canadian charity law.

### 6.5 In-kind contribution

Labor, expertise, materials, or services contributed at no fee.
Documented in Schedule A at the contributor's good-faith estimate of
fair-market value, for transparency and conflict-of-interest review,
even though in-kind contributions do not qualify for tax receipt.

### 6.6 Why no equity, no advance-purchase, no profit-share

The capital channels public-facing copy on the MTC website
([website/mtc/journey/index.html](../website/mtc/journey/index.html))
was reframed on 2026-05-04 to remove all "investor capital" and
"CSRA pre-sale" language. The covenant binds founding members to the
same constraint:

- **No equity** — no shares, units, or membership interest in any
  for-profit form (design doc §6.4 / §7.3).
- **No riba-bearing instrument** — no interest, no preferred return,
  no late fees structured as additional principal (design doc §7.5).
- **No advance-purchase, salam, or pre-sale** — Islam does not permit
  the sale of what one does not yet possess (*bayʿ mā laysa ʿindak*).
  See §6.1 above.
- **No profit-share** — even on a mushārakah-style basis, profit-share
  is not offered to founding members because the corpus is dedicated
  to the beneficiary class, not to capital partners.

A future yield-sharing channel may exist post-acquisition, designed as
a *membership benefit* under fresh Scholar Council review, and is not
in the scope of this covenant.

### 6.7 Anti-hīlah constraint

No combination of the above forms may be structured to produce, in
substance, an equity-like return, a debt-with-return, or a
preferred-creditor position. The Scholar Council shall review the
template Schedule A before any founding member signs, and shall
review any individual Schedule A whose terms deviate from the
template. **[scholar review]**

## 7. Removal and Withdrawal

### 7.1 Removal for cause

A founding member may be removed by the mutawalli (acting under the
qawī + amīn standard of design doc §4.1, with Scholar Council
attestation) for:

- **Breach of amānah** — including misuse of confidential information,
  unauthorized solicitation in MTC's name, or deception in any matter
  material to the covenant.
- **Manifest fisq** — public, sustained, and unrepented violation of
  shariʿah norms central to the household's qawī + amīn standing.
- **Sustained inability to participate** — meaning the qawī prong has
  become unmet by circumstance. This is not punitive; the covenant is
  closed gracefully.

### 7.2 Process

The removal process is modeled on the prophetic precedent cited in
design doc §4.1: **ʿUmar ibn al-Khaṭṭāb's dismissal of Saʿd from
leadership in Kufa**, in which investigators were dispatched to ask
the community about Saʿd's conduct *before* the dismissal decision
was finalized.

The MTC equivalent:

1. A mutawalli member, founding member, or Scholar Council member
   raises a concern, in writing.
2. The mutawalli appoints an investigator (who is not the complainant
   and is not the subject) to gather facts.
3. The subject is given written notice of the concern and an
   opportunity to respond.
4. Evidence is required per the Prophetic standard *"Produce your
   two witnesses or else the defendant is to take an oath"* (Sahih
   al-Bukhari Book 48 No. 837).
5. The mutawalli, by 2/3 of seated trustees with Scholar Council
   liaison present, decides.
6. The decision is documented per §8.

### 7.3 Voluntary withdrawal

A founding member may at any time withdraw from the covenant by
written notice to the mutawalli. Withdrawal is recorded; founding-
member designation ceases prospectively (the historical fact of
having been a founding member is not erased — that would be a
falsification of the record).

### 7.4 Treatment of capital on removal or withdrawal

- **Qard ḥasan** is repaid on the schedule documented in Schedule A.
  The schedule is not accelerated or decelerated by the removal or
  withdrawal.
- **Charitable donations and restricted donations** are not refundable.
- **In-kind contributions** are not reversed.

The treatment above is intentional: it ensures the covenant cannot be
used as a temporary parking arrangement for capital that is then
withdrawn at convenience, and it ensures the institution cannot use
removal as a pretext to extract additional capital. **[scholar review]**

## 8. Witnessing and Documentation

### 8.1 Scriptural mandate

**Al-Baqarah 2:282** (the longest verse in the Qur'an, design doc
§5.4) commands the documentation and witnessing of deferred financial
dealings: *"O you who have believed, when you contract a debt for a
specified term, write it down. And let a scribe write it between you
in justice... And bring to witness two witnesses from among your men..."*
The Prophetic standard *"Produce your two witnesses or else the
defendant is to take an oath"* (Sahih al-Bukhari Book 48 No. 837)
applies as the evidentiary backstop.

### 8.2 Signatures required

Each instance of this covenant requires:

- The founding member (head of household, with all adult household
  members co-signing where applicable).
- OGDEN as the initial wāqif (or its designated initial trustee
  acting on its behalf).
- **Two witnesses** to each signature.

### 8.3 Counterparts and archive

- One original is retained by the founding member.
- One original is archived by OGDEN, to be transferred into the
  records of the mutawalli upon its formal seating.
- Schedule A (the household-specific commitment letter) is treated
  with the same formality.

### 8.4 Re-attestation at deed of waqf

Once the deed of waqf is executed and the mutawalli is formally
seated, each founding-member covenant is **re-attested** — the
mutawalli countersigns, the Scholar Council attests, and the covenant
is incorporated by reference into the deed's record of founding
households. **[lawyer review]** — exact mechanic of incorporation is
counsel's call.

### 8.5 Scholar Council attestation of the template

**[open question]:** Before any founding member signs, the Scholar
Council attests the template version of this covenant once. OGDEN does
not enroll founding members against an unattested template. Recommended:
the attested template carries a version number, and any change to the
template requires re-attestation.

## 10. Appendix

### 10.1 Schedule A (per-household commitment letter — template)

Each founding-member covenant carries a Schedule A documenting the
specific terms agreed with that household. The template fields:

- Household name and signatories.
- Capital contribution form(s) elected from §6, with figures.
- Repayment schedule (if qard ḥasan).
- In-kind commitment description and good-faith fair-market value.
- Time / domain commitment per §4.4.
- Shūrā attendance floor per §4.2.
- Confidentiality scope (if elected per §4.5).
- Any household-specific conflicts of interest disclosed up front.
- Witness signatures per §8.2.

Schedule A is **private** to the household, OGDEN, and (post-seating)
the mutawalli. It is not surfaced in MTC public communications.
