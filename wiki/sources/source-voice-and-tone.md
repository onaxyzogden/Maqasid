## Summary

The voice-and-tone guide establishes how [[maqasid-os]] communicates with its users. It covers Islamic transliteration conventions, the naming of the Seven Maqasid with their Arabic equivalents, and editorial rules that preserve the application's Islamic identity without becoming preachy or exclusionary.

## Key Extractions

### Transliteration Conventions
Standard romanized forms used consistently across all UI surfaces:
- **Amanah** -- trust, stewardship
- **Sidq** -- truthfulness
- **Itqan** -- excellence, mastery
- **Tawakkul** -- reliance on Allah
- **Barakah** -- divine blessing, increase

### Seven Maqasid Naming
Each pillar uses both its English name and Arabic transliteration:
- Faith, Life, Intellect, Family, Wealth, Environment, Ummah
- Mapped to the classical [[maqasid-al-shariah]] framework (preservation of din, nafs, aql, nasl, mal, plus the extended objectives).

### Tone Rules
- **Purposeful, not preachy** -- Islamic framing should feel natural and integrated, not didactic.
- **Professional with warmth** -- the app serves serious life management; the tone reflects that gravity while remaining approachable.
- **Never genericize Islamic framing** -- do not strip religious context to make the product feel "more universal." The Islamic identity is the product's core differentiator, not an add-on.

## Notable Claims

- UI copy should prefer active voice and direct address ("You completed your Amanah check" over "The Amanah check has been completed").
- Error states and empty states are opportunities to reinforce purpose (e.g., "No tasks yet -- what will you build with the time Allah has given you?").
- Arabic terms appear in their transliterated form in running text but may appear in Arabic script (using Amiri font) in dedicated Islamic UI components like [[ceremony-gate-pattern]] surfaces.
