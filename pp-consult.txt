I'm designing a dashboard page called "The Prophetic Path" for MILOS, an Islamic life operating system. The page visualizes a Muslim's daily rhythm as a vertical timeline anchored by the five daily prayers plus transition moments (Isha/Rest, Tahajjud, Fajr, Morning start, Dhuhr, Midday labor, Asr, Maghrib). It is meant to answer: "Where am I in the sacred arc of the day, and what comes next?"

Current implementation (what you'd see in the attached screenshot):
- Large page heading "The Prophetic Path" with a subheading and a small "Prayer times for Testville, US" line
- Center vertical spine with alternating left/right cards at each time node
- Each card: an eyebrow (e.g. "LATE NIGHT"), time chip (e.g. "9:38 PM"), state badge ("CURRENT" or "NEXT"), a title (e.g. "Isha & Rest"), a body sentence, and small pillar chips (Faith / Soul / Wealth etc.)
- Small circular marker on the spine between nodes with a Lucide icon (moon, sparkles, sunrise, briefcase, sun, hardhat, sunset)
- Very faint ambient teal/gold wash gradients in the background corners
- ~8 nodes vertically → a 3–4 screen scroll on desktop
- "Current" and "Next" states use a tiny colored pill; otherwise card sizing is identical

What I see as problems in the current design:
1. MASSIVE whitespace: on a 900px-tall viewport only TWO cards are visible; one lives on the right half, the next on the left half, so ~50% of the horizontal space is permanently empty on any given row
2. Weak hierarchy: "Current" (happening now) and "Next" cards look almost identical — the state is communicated only via a tiny pill
3. No overview: the user cannot see the shape of their whole day at a glance — must scroll to know whether Dhuhr is near or far
4. The time chips ("9:38 PM", "3:00 AM · Last Third") compete with the state pills ("CURRENT", "NEXT") visually — too many same-sized eyebrow-line tokens
5. Ambient washes are so faint they feel decorative/accidental rather than purposeful
6. The alternating side pattern doesn't carry real meaning — it's just visual rhythm — but it costs half the horizontal space
7. Pillar chips (Faith/Soul/Life/Wealth/Ummah etc.) feel disconnected from the actual Maqasid color system used elsewhere in the app

Constraints / context:
- This is Islamic-covenant-grounded software. The page is reflective, not task-heavy. Clicking a node opens a prayer slide-up or submodule detail elsewhere.
- Light aesthetic: muted teal/gold/parchment palette, serif headings, generous line-height. This is intentional — NOT a productivity dashboard.
- We just redesigned the main dashboard with a three-tier Qalb(Intent)/Amal(Action)/Barakah(Impact) Islamic-semantic backbone and want the Prophetic Path to feel philosophically aligned.
- Mobile rendering is a concern — the two-column alternating spine probably collapses to a single column on mobile and that's fine.
- Users land here multiple times a day; the question "what phase am I in RIGHT NOW?" must be answered in under 1 second.

My two focused questions for you:

QUESTION 1 — Information density & hierarchy:
How would you restructure this timeline so (a) the user gets a full-day overview immediately (without scrolling), (b) the CURRENT moment is visually unmistakable from 10 feet away, and (c) the NEXT moment has secondary but clear prominence while the rest of the day recedes? I'm open to breaking the vertical-spine metaphor if something better serves the goal. Give me 2–3 concrete layout patterns with tradeoffs.

QUESTION 2 — Current-state prominence without loudness:
How do I make the active node dominant (a user opening the app must know instantly it's Isha time) without resorting to heavy-handed techniques that would break the contemplative aesthetic — i.e. NOT flashing borders, NOT bright saturated fills, NOT huge size jumps that feel like an alert? I want the visual weight of "sacred presence" — how do contemplative / editorial / meditation interfaces (Calm, Headspace, Kindle, Kinfolk-style editorial) solve this? Give me 3–4 specific techniques (typography, negative space, color, motion, or composition) that achieve calm prominence.

Reply in under 600 words total. Be specific — name the technique, how to apply it here, and what to avoid.
