## Fajr

### Before
*   Performed ablution (wudu) thoroughly as a prerequisite for prayer (Quran 5:6).
*   Cleaned his mouth with the tooth-stick (Siwak) upon waking for prayer (Sahih Bukhari 246 - Sahih). [1, 2]
*   Repeated the phrases of the Adhan as the Mu’adhdhin pronounced them (Sahih Muslim 746 - Sahih). [3]
*   Offered two light Sunnah rak'ahs after the dawn appeared (Sahih Muslim 1562 - Sahih). [4]
*   Recited Surah Al-Kafirun and Surah Al-Ikhlas in the two Sunnah rak'ahs (Sahih Muslim 1575 - Sahih). [5]
*   Lay down on his right side after the two Sunnah rak'ahs until the Iqama was called (Sahih Bukhari 1160 - Sahih). [6, 7]

### During
*   Led the compulsory congregational prayer consisting of two rak'ahs (Quran 17:78). [8]
*   Prolonged the recitation, typically between 60 to 100 verses of the Quran (Sahih Bukhari 541 - Sahih). [9]
*   Recited Surah As-Sajda and Surah Al-Insan during the Fajr prayer on Fridays (Sahih Bukhari 891 - Sahih). [10, 11]
*   Recited Qunut supplications after the ruku' (sometimes for a month) to invoke blessings or protection (Sahih Muslim 1428 - Sahih). [12, 13]

### After
*   Begged Allah for forgiveness (Istighfar) three times immediately after the salam (Sahih Muslim 1226 - Sahih). [14]
*   Recited: "O Allah! Thou art Peace, and peace comes from Thee..." (Sahih Muslim 1226 - Sahih). [14]
*   Remained sitting at his place of worship until the sun rose (Sahih Muslim 1413 - Sahih). [15, 16]

## Dhuhr

### Before
*   Offered four rak'ahs of Sunnah Rawatib in his house before the fard prayer (Sahih Muslim 1584 - Sahih). [8, 17]
*   Used the Siwak before performing wudu for the prayer session (Sahih Muslim 487 - Sahih). [18]
*   Delayed the prayer until the heat became cooler during periods of extreme midday temperature (Sahih Bukhari 533 - Sahih). [19, 20]

### During
*   Recited Al-Fatiha and another Surah in the first two rak'ahs, prolonging the first rak'ah (Sahih Muslim 908 - Sahih). [21]
*   Made his recitation occasionally audible to the companions (Sahih Muslim 909 - Sahih). [22]
*   Recited only Al-Fatiha in the final two rak'ahs (Sahih Muslim 909 - Sahih). [22]
*   Planted a spearheaded stick (Anza) in front of him as a Sutrah when praying in open spaces (Sahih Bukhari 478 - Sahih). [23, 24]

### After
*   Offered two rak'ahs of Sunnah Rawatib after returning home (Sahih Muslim 1584 - Sahih). [8, 17]
*   Recited the Tasbih (33x), Tahmid (33x), and Takbir (34x) to complete 100 praises (Sahih Muslim 1241 - Sahih). [25]
*   Recited post-prayer supplications: "None has the right to be worshipped but Allah... O Allah! No one can withhold what You give..." (Sahih Bukhari 805 - Sahih). [26, 27]

## Asr

### Before
*   Completed a thorough wudu, ensuring no part of the heels remained dry (Sahih Bukhari 164 - Sahih). [28, 29]
*   Recognized the extreme importance of Asr as the "Middle Prayer" (Quran 2:238). [30, 31]
*   Warned that deeds are annulled for one who intentionally leaves the Asr prayer (Sahih Bukhari 528 - Sahih). [32, 33]

### During
*   Recited Al-Fatiha and two additional Suras in the first two rak'ahs (Sahih Bukhari 726 - Sahih). [34]
*   Performed a silent recitation, which companions noted by the movement of his beard (Sahih Bukhari 713 - Sahih). [35-37]
*   Maintained a duration for bowing and prostration approximately equal to his standing (Sahih Muslim 953 - Sahih). [38]

### After
*   Prayed two rak'ahs in his house if he had missed the Sunnah of Dhuhr or as a regular habit in his home (Sahih Muslim 1815 - Sahih). [39, 40]
*   Extolled Allah 33 times each with SubhanAllah, Alhamdulillah, and Allahu Akbar (Sahih Muslim 1243 - Sahih). [41]
*   Recited: "O Allah, I seek refuge with You from the punishment of the grave..." (Sahih Muslim 1217 - Sahih). [42, 43]

## Maghrib

### Before
*   Hastened to break the fast at sunset if he had been fasting (Sahih Bukhari 1916 - Sahih). [44, 45]
*   Observed some companions praying two optional rak'ahs between the Adhan and Iqama and did not forbid it (Sahih Muslim 1820 - Sahih). [46, 47]

### During
*   Recited shorter Suras from the Mufassal section, or occasionally long ones like At-Tur or Al-Mursalat (Sahih Bukhari 732 - Sahih). [48-50]
*   Completed the three obligatory units of the Maghrib prayer (Quran 11:114). [51, 52]

### After
*   Offered two rak'ahs of Sunnah Rawatib in his house immediately after the fard (Sahih Muslim 1584 - Sahih). [8, 17]
*   Recited: "None has the right to be worshipped but Allah... to Him belong all bounties..." (Sahih Muslim 1235 - Sahih). [53]
*   Recited the last two verses of Surah Al-Baqarah during the night (Sahih Bukhari 4723 - Sahih). [54]

## Isha

### Before
*   Disliked sleeping before the Isha prayer or engaging in idle talk after it (Sahih Bukhari 543 - Sahih). [55-57]
*   Would pray earlier if people gathered quickly or delay if they were slow to assemble (Sahih Bukhari 542 - Sahih). [58, 59]

### During
*   Recited Surahs of medium length, such as Al-Inshiqaq or At-Tin (Sahih Bukhari 733 - Sahih). [49, 60, 61]
*   Prostrated upon reaching the verse of prostration (Sajdah) in the Surah being recited (Sahih Muslim 1198 - Sahih). [49, 62]

### After
*   Offered two rak'ahs of Sunnah Rawatib in his house (Sahih Muslim 1584 - Sahih). [8, 17]
*   Recited Ayat al-Kursi before going to bed for protection (Sahih Bukhari 2311 - Sahih). [63, 64]
*   Recited Surah Al-Kafirun before sleeping to remain free from polytheism (Sahih Bukhari 109:1 - Sahih). [65]

## Tahajjud

### Before
*   Woke up during the last third of the night and rubbed the traces of sleep from his face (Sahih Bukhari 183 - Sahih). [66-68]
*   Recited the final ten verses of Surah Aal-Imran upon waking (Sahih Muslim 1672 - Sahih). [66-69]
*   Cleaned his mouth thoroughly with the Siwak (Sahih Muslim 491 - Sahih). [1, 2, 70]
*   Performed a perfect and light ablution from a hanging water-skin (Sahih Bukhari 183 - Sahih). [66, 68, 69]

### During
*   Commenced the night prayer session with two short rak'ahs (Sahih Muslim 1689 - Sahih). [71]
*   Prayed in pairs of two rak'ahs (Sahih Muslim 1632 - Sahih). [72, 73]
*   Prolonged the standing (Qiyam) so much that his feet would sometimes swell (Sahih Bukhari 1130 - Sahih). [74]
*   Recited the Quran leisurely, pausing to ask for mercy or seek refuge when reciting corresponding verses (Sahih Muslim 1697 - Sahih). [75]
*   Supplicated during the opening of the prayer: "O Allah, Lord of Gabriel, Michael, and Israfil..." (Sahih Muslim 1694 - Sahih). [76]
*   Increased his supplication during the prostration (Sujud), as it is the closest a servant is to Allah (Sahih Muslim 482 - Sahih). [77]

### After
*   Concluded the night prayer with a single rak'ah of Witr as the final prayer (Sahih Muslim 1641 - Sahih). [78-80]
*   Supplicated with "The Light Du'a": "O Allah! place light in my heart, light in my hearing..." (Sahih Muslim 1671 - Sahih). [81-83]
